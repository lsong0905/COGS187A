UCSD DINING APPLICATION | ANDROID

Android Mobile Application

Click here to check out android files on gitHub!

Date

09/21/2015 - 11/30/2015

Purpose

Developing an android mobile application

Required Skill

Android and knowledge of snchronization and linking to database

Team Member

Leo Song, Julie Yao, Jason Wong and Min Nguyen



Objective

This is an android mibile application for dinning hall. It provides four features: Showing today's menu, searching for certain menu, wishlisting menu and tracking calories and price. There is already existing UCSD dinning application, but it does not provide any feature mentioned above. So, our group has built this application to provide convenience to students who may need such features.


Features

1. Dining Halls
It shows today's menu in every dining hall.

2. Search
It searches items if they are available today.

3. Wishlist.
It stores selected items, and show if they are availabe today.

4. Tracker
It tracks selected items' calories and prices.

Development Process


1. Meeting with our customer.

Our group as a developer was assigned to a customer group. We talked with our customer what kind of application they want us to build and why they want to have the application.


2. User Stories

Our group came up with 14 possible user stories that students may face using our application. Then, we priotized the user stories in descending order. (the one with 14 would be the most important one; the one with 1 would be the least important one) We decided the priority after discussing with our customer. So, it guided us which feature(functionality) should be implemented first.


3. Continuous feedback from our customer.

We had a meeting with our customer at least once a week. We talked about the tasks we were currently working on, and what would be the next task. The feedback from our customer generally guided us how to proceed further.M


4. Designing low-fidelity prototyping.

Our group started to prototype basic layout of the application.



5. Parsing and storing into database

Our group needed to parse information everyday from the UCSD hds website. Once we parse every menu in every dining hall, we store the data into our database(Parse.com). Then, we retrieve data everyday and render it on our application.


6. Storing user data(wishlist and conter)

When a user type select an item, the item is stored in our database. Then, when the user clicks either "wishlist" or "counter," it retrieves every menu stored as "wishlist" or "counter" in our database.

Information Architecture

Date : Feb. 2015

Required Skills: Balsamiq and Excel.

Team Member : Leo Song and Lynn Hao



Objective

Purpose : Understanding what is involved in making a usable information architecture and redesigning the information architecture and making a reasonable high wirefram of the key pages on the site.


Process

1. Interview and Persona Table

First, we came upt with several different personas; current undergraduate student, prospective graduate student, professor and parents. Then, we interviewed them to see which use cases are deriven from them.



2. Creating Use Cases

We made a table that shows different use cases for different personas. This table helped us to understand and come up with which features are necessary for re-designing website.




3. Analyzing different examples

We analyzed five different universities(UZ, NUST, AU, WUA, and CUT). Then, we compared the data from the universities with our website, Alaska University (e.g which navigation bar has the most frequency).




4. Competitive analysis (Second-level Navigation)

We calculated cost and distance from main page to second level page in Alaska Univ. website and our redesigned website




5. Prototyping redesigned website




6. Hardest decision

After having finished redesigning the website, we discussed the difficult design decisions that we had to make in the process of implementation.
